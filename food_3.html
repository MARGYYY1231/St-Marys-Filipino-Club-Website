Filipino foods




Hello there! I will talk about some of filipino foods. These are some filipino foods we reccomend you to try down below. If you click on the image it will lead to where the image is from. To find out more about other filipino foods click here.


Adobo

Philippine adobo has soft delicious chicken, also it has a characteristically salty and sour (and often sweet) taste. Adobo's main ingredients are Paprika, salt, garlic, and vinegar.

Lechon

Lechon is one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines. The slowly-roasted suckling pig is usually stuffed with lemongrass, tamarind, garlic, onions, and chives, and is then roasted on a large bamboo spit over an open fire.

Halo-Halo

Halo-halo is the ultimate summertime treat. It is a layered dessert consisting of sweetened beans, fruits, shaved ice drizzled with evaporated milk, and ice cream.

Sisig

Sisig makes a great party appetizer as well as a hearty dinner entree. It has a delicious medley of velvety soft pork bits, onions, and chili peppers tossed in tangy and savory dressing, it's spicy, tasty,

Palabok

Pancit Palabok is another popular and favorite dish among Filipinos.It usually involves rice noodles, thick yellow-orange sauce and various toppings like shrimps, flakes of smoked fish, pork chicharon, hard boiled eggs, spring onions, squid rings, fried garlic.

Tocino

Tocino is a thinly sliced pork shoulder cured in salt and sugar. It is a sweet, chewy Filipino breakfast meat that results is amazing, especially when paired with some garlic fried rice and egg

Filipino spaghetti

Filipino spaghetti are sweet, the noodles are combined with ground meat, hot dogs, banana ketchup, and sugar.

Puto

To make Adobo white vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves, are used to create a marinade.
